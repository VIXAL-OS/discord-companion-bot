# MTG Tarot — Complete System Summary

## The Dual-Deck Architecture

This MTG Tarot system uses two parallel 92-card decks that explore the same color mechanics through opposite philosophical lenses:

### Sephirothic Engine (Path of Harmony)
- **Major Arcana:** 22 colorless cards representing universal archetypes
- **Minor Arcana:** 5 suits from allied color pairs (WU, BR, RG, GW, UB)
- **Theme:** Colors working together, synergy, cooperation, idealized potential

### Qliphothic Engine (Path of Shells)  
- **Major Arcana:** 22 mono-colored cards (2 per card from each color + colorless)
- **Minor Arcana:** 5 suits from enemy color pairs (WB, UR, BG, RW, GU)
- **Theme:** Colors in tension, transformation through conflict, shadow work

---

## Complete Suit Structure

Each suit has 14 cards:
- **Ace through Ten:** Numbered cards showing progression
- **Face Cards (11-14):** Envoy/Challenger, Champion/Adversary, Steward/Witness, Architect/Alchemist

Each card position has 3 tiers:
- **Primary:** Optimal choice for readings
- **Alt:** Alternative with different flavor
- **Budget:** Accessible option

## Thematic Structure

### Ace: Beginning
The first spark. Pure potential in the suit's domain.

### Two: Partnership  
Duality emerging. First relationship within the suit's energy.

### Three: Expansion
Growth beyond pair. Community/network forming.

### Four: Foundation
Stability achieved. Structure established.

### Five: Challenge
First real test. Conflict or difficulty.

### Six: Integration
Resolution of Five. Balance restored or achieved.

### Seven: Testing Limits
Pushing boundaries. Edge of mastery or excess.

### Eight: Mastery
Peak competence. Excellence in the suit's domain.

### Nine: Flourishing
Abundance. The suit at its peak expression.

### Ten: Legacy
Completion. What the suit builds or leaves behind.

### Face Cards

**Sephirothic (Allied) Titles:**
- 11: The Envoy — herald, invitation, one who brings
- 12: The Champion — defender, peak expression, embodiment  
- 13: The Steward — keeper, maintainer, one who sustains
- 14: The Architect — designer, creator, one who builds

**Qliphothic (Enemy) Titles:**
- 11: The Challenger — tests, questions, necessary opposition
- 12: The Adversary — resists, opposes, that which must be overcome
- 13: The Witness — observes, records, one who remembers
- 14: The Alchemist — transforms, transmutes, works in the shadows

## Reading Framework

### Single Card
Draw one card. Consider upright meaning (harmony with suit's energy) and inverted meaning (shadow of that energy).

### Two-Card Opposition
Draw from Sephirothic and Qliphothic decks. The pair shows light and shadow of the same question.

### Five-Color Spread  
One card from each color's suit pair. Shows how all five aspects of the psyche relate to the question.

### Major-Minor Bridge
Draw one Major Arcana from each deck, then the Minor Arcana that bridges them. Shows universal archetype manifesting in specific energy.

## Design Principles Applied

1. **Color Identity Strict:** All cards match their suit's exact two-color pair
2. **Deduplication:** No card appears twice within the same suit
3. **Mechanical Resonance:** Card abilities reflect position meanings
4. **Inverted Meanings:** Each position has a shadow interpretation
5. **Tiered Accessibility:** Primary/Alt/Budget options for all positions
6. **Thematic Consistency:** Ravnica guilds inform but don't constrain flavor
